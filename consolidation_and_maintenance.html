Relationship with different groups

Monarchy
The King Victor Emmanuel III likes Mussolini's “strong figure who could impose order over Italy” as well as the “patriotism displayed by the Blackshirts”27. Nevertheless, the king is against the violence used by Mussolini to impose order28. Victor Emmanuel allows Mussolini to do mostly anything he wanted from assassinating rivals to passing laws. But the King refuses “to add the fasces symbol to the flag” because he sees it as an offense to his family29. Another actions took by the King against Mussolini is his refusal to enter the World War II because Italy is not ready30. The lack of actions from Victor Emmanuel shows his lack of interest in governing a country.

Catholic Church
The relations between the Catholic Church and Mussolini are tensed. Fascists radicals, including Mussolini, believe the Church is "a barrier to modernity and progress"31. On the other hand, the Church believes "Fascism [...] is a threat to the social order"32.

Although the two groups have contradicting ideas about each other, in 1929, the Lateran Treaty is created33. This treaty is a compromised between the Catholic Church and the state. As a result, the Church does not get involved in politics, in exchange, the Church has control over the education34. In addition, the Pope Pius XI receives cash from the state35.

Methods Used

Use of Propaganda
Some methods of propaganda is used on the education of young people. Like most dictators, Mussolini believes that, to achieve unconditional support from the Italians, he needs to teach them his ideology at their youngest age. For example, children would pray at school everyday36. However, children are not praying for God like in the Catholic religion, they are praying for Mussolini: “I believe in the genius of Mussolini”37. In general, people's beliefs are deeply linked to the education they received as a child. Therefore, Mussolini hopes to control the Italian population by exposing the youths to fascist ideologies. It should have had a powerful impact on the population because the youths are educated to lift Mussolini to the status of God. However, "40% of 4-18 years-old manage to avoid membership" mostly due to the schools' resistance to the fascist government, especially in "private and Catholic schools"38.


Propaganda is also used on adult by the creation of Dopolavoro: after work clubs that aim to take control of the adult population39. In these clubs, adults could benefit from various activities, which are linked to fascism, to enjoy their evenings40. Furthermore, by encouraging adults to join these clubs, Mussolini knows the Italians are exposed to his ideology rather than to take part in rebellious activities. However, the Dopolavoro are not as successful as intended because the organizations quickly let go of their aim of propagating fascist ideologies41. As a failure, the Dopolavoro shows the lack of control Mussolini had over the population.

Suppression of opposition
During the rise to power of Mussolini, people involved in a fascist secret organization created by Mussolini, attack a socialist leader who is advocating for the violence of the fascist party and fraudulent elections42. That event is named the Matteotti Incident because the murdered man's name is Giacomo Matteotti43. The population suspects Mussolini to have orchestrated this event and wants his dismissal44. However, Mussolini has the support of the pope and the King, therefore he keeps his position45. In order to stop the population from protesting and investigating into the Matteotti incident, Mussolini dismisses three fascist ministers46.

In 1927, a secret police force named OVRA (Oragnization for Vigilance and Repression of Anti-Fascism) is created47. Its main purpose is to suppress political opponents48. Like most dictator, Mussolini uses OVRA to create fear within the population to maintain order
