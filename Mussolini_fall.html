Conditions in Italy post-WW1

World War II
Although Mussolini and Hitler have signed the Pact of Steel, Hitler invades Poland before the three years asked my Mussolini97. Therefore, Italy only joins the war on June 10, 194098. During the war, Italians suffer an “unrelieved succession of military disasters” and must be helped by Germany several times rather than to help them99.

Public Opinion
On July 24 and 25, 1943, the Fascist Grand Council votes for the king to dismiss Mussolini and to resume full power100. As a result, “statues of Mussolini [are] torn down, Fascist symbols [are] removed, and political prisoners [are] released”101. This shows the anger of the population towards Mussolini's regime.

Capture
Mussolini sets up a “new fascist government in north-easter Italy” under Hitler's commandment102. This demonstrates the power Hitler gains over Mussolini becase he is able to control him. In April 1945, Mussolini flees to Milan with his government, and tries to cross the German border but he is caught and shot103. The bodies of Mussolini and of his government are then hung by their feet “service station in front of huge festive crowds”104. This suggests a will to humiliate Mussolini and his government.
